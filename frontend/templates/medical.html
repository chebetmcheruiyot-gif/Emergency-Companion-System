MEDICAL RESPONSE

Follow these guidelines while waiting for emergency services. SHELLY is here to help — tap the button below.

CPR Protocol

  • 1 Check for response — tap shoulders, shout.
  • 2 Call for help. Lay on firm flat surface.
  • 3 Push hard & fast — centre of chest.
  • 4 100–120 compressions per minute.
  • 5 Allow chest to fully recoil.

Severe Bleeding

  • Pressure: Apply firm direct pressure with clean cloth.
  • Elevate: Raise the limb above heart level.
  • Don't remove: Add more cloth if it soaks through.
  • Tourniquet: Only if bleeding is life-threatening.

Burns

  • 1st Degree: Cool water for 20 mins. No ice.
  • 2nd Degree: Do not pop blisters. Cover loosely.
  • 3rd Degree: Call 999. Do not remove clothing.
  • Chemical: Flush with water for at least 20 mins.

Unconscious Person

  • Check: Tap shoulders and shout their name.
  • Breathing: Look, listen and feel for breath.
  • Recovery: If breathing — recovery position.
  • CPR: If not breathing — start CPR immediately.

Choking

  • Ask: "Are you choking?" — if yes, act fast.
  • 5 Back Blows: Firm blows between shoulder blades.
  • 5 Abdominal Thrusts: Heimlich maneuver.
  • Repeat: Alternate until object dislodges.

Seizure

  • Don't restrain: Never hold the person down.
  • Clear area: Remove hard or sharp objects nearby.
  • Cushion head: Place something soft under head.
  • Call 999: If seizure exceeds 5 minutes.
